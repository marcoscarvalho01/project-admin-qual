PROJECT OVERVIEW

Welcome to the Midas R&R!

Your goal is to evaluate the quality, accuracy, and formatting of financial Q&A pairs generated by other workers based on SEC 10-K and 10-Q reports. Since this data will be used to train high-level models (our Golden Dataset), absolute accuracy is critical.

You will review tasks from Simple Prompts, Complex Prompts, and Multi-document Complex Prompts and, then, rate both the initial submission and the final submission (after your edits).

A typical review takes around 15 to 25 minutes to complete, so scope your work within that time frame (some heavy-editing tasks are allowed to exceed this time if you turn a BAD submission into an Amazing one!)

GROUND RULES

WHENEVER POSSIBLE, EDIT THE RESPONSE. We expect you to edit the workers submission to make it GOOD, not only point us out where the inputs should be edited.

IF THE SUBMISSION WOULD TAKE TOO LONG TO EDIT, RATE IT AS BAD. In this scenario, the best approach is to rate both the initial and final submissions as BAD and justify it in the comments section.

ALWAYS PROVIDE JUSTIFICATIONS. Even when the response is initially GOOD, please, tell us why in the comments. If you rated it as OK or BAD initially, please, explain why and tell us what you edited to fix the submission.

REVIEW STEPS

  1. Verify the Source(s): Open the provided SEC EDGAR link(s) and file(s). Ensure the uploaded document(s) match(es) the requested company(ies) and time period.
  2. Evaluate the Prompt: Is it atomic, factual, and strictly financial? Does it match the assigned type (Simple vs. Complex vs Multi-document)?
    • Simple Prompts: Should require NO math. Just information retrieval.
    • Complex Prompts: Must require synthesizing information from multiple places OR performing financial calculations.
    • Multi-document: Complex Prompts that span across multiple documents, utilizing every single pre-assigned document to build the question.
  3. Fact-Check the Answer (Ground Truth): Cross-reference the financial figures, tables, and narrative in the worker's response with their Supporting Facts and the SEC document. Ensure exact formatting (e.g., proper use of units like "millions", explicit timeframes).
  4. Audit the Facts and Rationale:
    • Supporting Facts: Did they copy-paste the text verbatim from the PDF? Are the page numbers and sections accurate?
    • Rationale (For Complex Prompts): Did they explicitly state the formula and show the step-by-step arithmetic?

GRADING RUBRIC

You must rate the overall submission on a 3-point scale:

  • GOOD: Flawless or minor errors/edits only. A slight typo in the rationale, a minor formatting issue in the answer (e.g., forgot a comma), or slightly clunky phrasing. The financial data is 100% accurate and required only a few/minor edits.
  • OK: Required some major edits. The response missed a secondary constraint, or the citation is formatted incorrectly (e.g., paraphrased instead of verbatim). Financial data is still accurate, but requires editing. If you are in doubt, mark OK in the final submission rating to allow Admins to resolve the matter.
  • BAD: Unusable or major edits were required. Hallucinated financial numbers, completely missed the prompt's core intent, failed the math in a Complex Prompt, or included PII are automatically rated as BAD initially, but you can (and should) try to make it a GOOD submission.

If you rate a task as OK or BAD, you will be required to select the error categories and provide written feedback explaining your rating.

WRITING FACTUAL PROMPTS

A good prompt is the heart of this project, so let's make sure yours is built to last! The goal here is to write the kind of financial-related rigorous question that a real analyst would ask before or while reviewing an SEC filing. It must be atomic, specific, grounded in the document, and with one clear correct answer (the Ground Truth).

COMPLEX PROMPTS

A Complex Prompt is a question that cannot be answered by finding a single sentence or table cell. Instead, the answer to this question MUST satisfy ONE OR MORE of the following conditions:

  • Computing a number that is not explicitly stated (e.g., calculating a margin, a growth rate, a ratio)
  • Combining information from multiple sections of the same document (e.g., using a number from a table on page 40 and a footnote clarification on page 43)
  • Applying financial domain knowledge to interpret the data (e.g., "exclude a one-time charge" from a loss)

WRITING PERFECT ANSWERS

  1. Be direct and concise
  2. Use full sentences
  3. Be fanatical about units and formats
  4. Always try to ground your answer in time
  5. Respect the PII Rules strictly

IMPORTANT: By convention, for this project, any percentage data must be rounded to the nearest first decimal place and money figures must be rounded to the nearest integer.

HOW TO PROVIDE SUPPORTING FACTS

In the task workflow, you will see a specific form to fill out for each fact you need to prove your answer. You can add as many facts as you need by clicking the "Add" button.

  1. Evidence (Verbatim Copy-Paste Only!)

WRITING YOUR RATIONALES

The Rationale is the step-by-step logic that connects your raw Supporting Facts to your Perfect Answer.

  1. Spell Out the Formula
  2. Show the Step-by-Step Math (Including Conversions)
  3. Explain the "Why" (The Hidden Logic)
